  - Basic metrics: weight, waist, body fat estimate, training schedule, work hours, sleep

    - Handouts & infographics: portion guides, sample meal plans, grocery lists  
    - Posters around gym: hydration, pre/post-workout snack ideas, protein targets

      - Week 3: hydration target  
      - Week 4: pre/post-workout fueling

    - Individualized calorie & macro targets

    - Progress tracking: weight, measurements, performance, adherence  
    - Integration with trainers’ programming (e.g., carb timing around heavy days)

    - Protein: 1.6–2.2 g/kg body weight  
    - Fat: 0.6–1.0 g/kg  
    - Carbs: fill remaining calories based on goal & training volume  
  - **Goal-based guidelines**  
    - Fat loss: 300–500 kcal/day deficit, higher protein, moderate carbs around training  
    - Muscle gain: 200–300 kcal/day surplus, high protein, higher carbs, especially pre/post-workout  
    - General health: maintenance calories, focus on food quality & consistency  
  - **Pre-/post-workout**  
    - Pre (60–120 min): 20–40 g protein + 25–60 g carbs, low fat  
    - Post (0–2 hrs): 20–40 g protein + 30–80 g carbs, low–moderate fat  

- **Delivery & integration in the gym**  
  - New-member orientation includes a 10–15 min nutrition briefing + basic guide  
  - Trainers use simple scripts to reinforce nutrition habits with clients  
  - Optional body-composition checks every 4–6 weeks  
  - Partner with a local RD for referrals and to cover medical/complex cases  

- **Metrics for success**  
  - Member retention and upsell rate to coaching  
  - Average attendance at seminars/groups  
  - % of members hitting protein/habit targets (self-reported)  
  - Before/after performance and body-composition snapshots  

2) Monitoring server performance – concise framework  
If you’re running this program on a digital platform (app/website), you need basic observability:

- **Key metrics**  
  - CPU, memory, disk I/O, network throughput  
  - Request rate, error rate, latency (p95/p99)  
  - DB metrics: query time, connections, slow queries  
  - Uptime & availability per service  

- **Tools**  
  - Cloud-native (CloudWatch, Stackdriver, Azure Monitor) or Prometheus + Grafana  
  - APM: New Relic, Datadog, Dynatrace, or OpenTelemetry stack  
  - Centralized logs: ELK/EFK stack, Datadog Logs, Splunk  

- **Alerting**  
  - Threshold & anomaly-based alerts on:  
    - High CPU/memory  
    - Elevated error rate (4xx/5xx)  
    - Latency spikes  
    - DB saturation  
  - On-call rotation with escalation policy  

- **Dashboards**  
  - High-level “health” dashboard for management  
  - Detailed service dashboards for engineers  
  - Business KPIs: active users, signups, conversion to paid nutrition services  

- **Practices**  
  - Capacity planning based on trends  
  - Load testing before major launches  
  - Post-incident reviews with clear action items
